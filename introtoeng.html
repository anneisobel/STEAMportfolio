Introduction to Engineering

An Online STEAM Unit Written to Introduce 6th Graders to Engineering

This STEAM unit was promoted as a special STEAM workshop for middle schoolers with the purpose of introducing the students to engineering. It was taught to a group of sixth graders over four 90-minute sessions throughout a two-week period. Each session was taught online over Zoom. Students participated from home or from church using computers, iPads, and/or iPhones.

Problem Scenario

Look for a problem you can solve in your life or around you. Design a solution and present it to your classmates.

Driving Questions

  1. What is engineering?
  2. How do engineers view the world?

Reflection: Designing the Unit

This unit was written with the goal of introducing students to engineering and to the engineering design process. As such, the problem scenario was purposely written to be vague and very open. The first step in the engineering design process is to "identify the problem." One of the goals of this unit was to teach students to view the world as engineers do and learn how to identify problems. Therefore, no specific problem was presented in the problem scenario. Students were responsible for discovering problems to solve.

The driving questions were written to prompt students to "Think like an engineer!" and were used as reminders throughout the workshop as students worked through the engineering design process.
